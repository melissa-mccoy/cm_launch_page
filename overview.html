Problem

Accessing quality healthcare is challenging in South Africa and throughout the continent.

Inaccessible

Doctors are often only available during work hours & urban sprawl+heavy traffic can make a trip to the doctor take the entire day. Charl, a business consultant in Johannesburg, complained it took her over 4 hours to see his primary care physician just to get a specialist referral. Elani, a working mother, said, while it sometimes proves unnecessary, she misses work & takes her newborn to the doctor for all his ailments, wanting to be sure nothing serious is missed. She's starting to think it might be impossible to continue working and care for her children.

Expensive

According to the South African National Treasury, the per capita expenditure for medical schemes increased 158%, in real terms, from 1990 and 2000 while benefits declined consistently. And the World Bank shows that per capita health expenditure has more than doubled since 2000. This is making quality healthcare increasingly unattainable for South Africa's rising middle class. Because of our minimal overhead costs, we are able to pay doctors private care salaries but charge patients significantly less.

Sensitive

There are several health issues that patients find difficult to share with friends & family and wish not to discuss with their family doctors. This unfortunately leaves many people without proper care & treatment for their ailments such as:

  • Mental Health Issues: A Mental Health and Poverty Research Program study found that 16.5% of South African adults suffer from mental illness. For fear of social ostracism, many keep their mental illness secret instead of seeking the much needed medical attention.
  • Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STDs) & HIV/AIDs: According to the Department of Health, widespread lack of knowledge, resistance, or popular misconceptions regarding STDs are a huge reason why they remain a major health problem. Less than 20% have received sexual education by age 18.
  • Sexual Violence: The rate of sexual violence in South Africa is among the highest in the world. According to the South African Medical Research Council (SAMRC) in a 2009 study, over 40% of South African women are raped at least once in their lifetimes but only 1 in 9 are reported. Women often don't know where to turn for healthcare related to such incidents.

Our patients can choose to turn video off and not disclose their name to feel safe when discussing their issues with or seeking a specialist referral from one of our doctors.

Scarce

There are not enough doctors to meet demand. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), in South Africa, there are only 0.77 doctors for every thousand people, compared with an average of 3.5 in developed countries and over two-thirds of existing doctors live in urban areas. There are several ways that our video consultations can help this issue:

  • Captures Doctor Idle Time: 73% of private sector doctors surveyed said they often have openings in their day - perhaps there's a 30min break between patients or a patient has canceled. This "idle" doctor time can be captured by our platform as doctors can just jump online whenever they're free and serve patients who are in need.
  • Captures Doctor Good Will: 45% of urban doctors surveyed said they would provide consultations to rural patients at a discounted rate as a form of giving back, but only 3% would if they had to travel to the rural location. This desire to serve can be captured by our platform and provide more resources to rural patients.
  • Makes Public Sector More Attractive: 65% of public sector doctors said they'd like to serve on the platform in off hours to earn extra money. Perhaps more doctors would serve in the public sector knowing they have this additionally income option?

Doctor Video Consultations

ConnectMed Video Consultations allow patients to see a doctor anytime, anywhere via our online platform.

How it Works

You just come to our site, enter your symptoms, and with minimal wait time you'll be talking with a doctor who will diagnose you, provide you with a prescription, and have you on your way within 30 minutes flat. You'll pay per consult at a rate less than the average South African private doctor charges now and you'll get a full refund if you're not completely satisfied.


How We Help

Our doctors can diagnose over 30 common ailments ranging from cold & flu to urinary tract infections. Our liability analysis and discussions with South African medical bodies also confirms that video-based diagnosis is acceptable to the system, just as it is in the US & UK.

Common Ailments
Cough, Cold, Flu & Allergies

Upper respiratory infections are the broad category for flu, sinus infections, bronchitis, runny noses, coughs and colds. These common conditions can often be diagnosed through ConnectMed, keeping you out of the doctorâ€™s office and away from unnecessary germs.

Sore Throat

A sore throat is a very common symptom which could be an indicator of a series of ailments, including many that can often be treated via our video consultations. It can be difficult for a patient to know if they have a Cold, Allergies, Strep Throat, or Tonsillitis unless they visit with a doctor. Our doctors are trained to evaluate your symptoms and, depending on the clinical circumstances, diagnose and treat the various causes of a sore throat specifically through a video visit.

Urinary Tract Infections

Urinary tract infections (UTIs) are a very common condition. In fact, more often than not, when patients come to a doctor they have already experienced the symptoms and have been diagnosed with this condition before. While physicians will sometimes order a urinalysis, in many cases a urinalysis is not necessary to make a diagnosis. Either way, the physician may want to begin treatment before the urinalysis results come back. This is because a urine culture can take 48 hours before it starts growing any bacteria.

Minor Injuries

Sports and exercise activities are an important part of any healthy lifestyle. But with increased activity comes the increased risk of injury. In the absence of an obviously severe injury like a broken bone, most doctorâ€™s visits concerning sports injuries are patients looking for reassurance that the injury is not more severe. Sports injuries often fall into either a minor category (ankle sprains, wrist sprains, shoulder or rib soreness) or a severe category (broken bone, severely torn ligament/tendon).

Skin Conditions

Common conditions include rashes, STDs, poison ivy, insect bites, cold sores, psoriasis, bites, contact dermatitis, scrapes, allergies, hives, sores and boils.These conditions are most often diagnosable by visual inspection. An important component of dermatological assessment is recognizing patterns, features, signs and symptoms. Using your computer camera through our platform, you can upload multiple photos safely and directly to your physician who can also store them to assess changes over time. The photos are transmitted with lossless compression giving your doctor a precise view of the tissue.

Diarrhea & Vomiting

Diagnosing the cause and determining appropriate treatment of diarrhea typically begins with very focused clinical questions. Your physician may recommend a special diet, over the counter and/or prescription medications, or follow-up care. Our doctor are often able to determine the cause and provide treatment for vomiting during a video consultation by reviewing your medical history, asking questions, and performing an exam.

Diagnosing the condition and severity often begins with questions about pain, discharge and previous eye damage, and the collection of a patientâ€™s medical history. Then on camera, the physician can have the patient do several eye maneuvers and ask for still photographs to fully assess the eye.

Intelligent Medical Tools

We're continuously developing machine learning-based medical tools that will assist in remote care.

Symptom Check & Triage

Know what care you need & when.

We're developing a patient-facing application that advises patients on if and how urgently they need medical care based on their symptoms and history (a.k.a non-emergency triage). We've used UK patient data for initial training of the machine learning model but the algorithm will further be trained on South African patient data produced from our platform, making it even more accurate for the local context.

Vital Signs Detection

Get your vitals just from your webcam.

We're optimizing currently existing machine learning-based algorithms that extract pulse & respiratory rate via facial video footage to work better with all skin tones. This will provide doctors with patient vital sign information during video consultation to aid in their diagnosis. We'll likely also create a patient-facing tool that will allow patients to take their own vital signs for personal interest.

More Machine Learing Tools

Many more in development.

While digitized patient data is generally hard to come by, digitized African patient data is virtually non-existent given the lack of electronic medical records. This is where our platform shows tremendous value - it collects & anonymizes patient information which can be used to develop a multitude of machine learning-based medical tools that are highly accurate and generalizable to any local context.

Mission

We seek to enable all Africans to live healthier, longer, and happier lives - no matter their location or income level.


By first targeting middle & upper income groups, we will generate the trust & traction needed to service lower income populations through the below strategies.

Affordable Pricing

In this context, we will lower the consultation price to an affordable rate, using higher income profits to subsidize service, and are also developing a network of doctors generously willing to accept discounted payment for serving these communities.


Connectivity Partner

While over 40% of South African households have at least one member with access to Internet at home or elsewhere, it is unlikely that lower income urban and rural patients will have the ability to access ConnectMed services thorough the Web. Therefore, we are in discussions with partner organizations who can provide the connectivity & infrastructure needed through "healthcare hubs".


Optimized Technology

While our web platform has already been designed with low bandwidth, low computationally powerful machines, and data cost sensitivity optimizations, we are working on further improving this functionality. Additionally we are developing a native Android application given the majority of lower income households have connectivity via mobile phones.



Business Potential

We are a socially-minded for-profit company headquartered in South Africa with huge business potential.


Market Size

Starting in South Africa

Using the South African Advertising Research Foundation's 2012 Living Standard Measure (SAARF LSM), the most widely used marketing research tool in Southern Africa, we've defined our target market as the LSM 7-10 segments which represent households that earn over R10255 (~$830 USD) monthly, live in urban environments, and own or have unlimited access to a computer+internet. This group earns 65% of total income, comprises 20% of the population, and is interested in using telemedicine (over 70% confirmed this in our market surveys).


Expansion to the Continent

Expansion to other African countries with substantial LSM 7-10 populations like Nigeria and Kenya will naturally follow at least a year after establishing a robust and replicable model in South Africa. Entry into non-Anglophone Africa will also be kicked off with entry into Senegal. The market potential for the continent is over $6 billion and given South Africa's prominence, adoption here will likely mean smooth adoption elsewhere.


Monetization Strategy


While we see our data and machine learning tools being a large revenue generator, we've done revenue & cost projections focusing solely on our doctor video consultations which alone will allow us to break-even in the second year and reach over $10 million USD in profit by Year 5. The consultation price and doctor payment fee were decided upon through customer & doctor surveys and interviews.
